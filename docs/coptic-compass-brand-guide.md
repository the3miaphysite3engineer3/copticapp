## 01. Brand Foundation

### Who We Are

Coptic Compass is a digital home for Coptology. It brings together a
searchable dictionary, structured grammar lessons, publications, Shenute AI,
OCR-adjacent workflows, learner tools, and public APIs into one coherent
platform.

The project is built by Copts for Copts, while remaining useful to everyone who
studies the Coptic language: students, teachers, clergy, scholars, linguists,
archaeologists, software developers, and independent learners.

Coptic Compass is not just a portfolio, blog, or isolated dictionary. It is a
publisher and learning platform with a scholarly center of gravity and a modern
technical surface.

Coptic Compass is the publisher of the seven works currently listed in the
publications catalog. The publication page, covers, metadata, descriptions,
Open Graph cards, and public references should treat Coptic Compass as the
publisher imprint for those works.

### Purpose

To make Coptic more searchable, learnable, teachable, and usable in modern
digital contexts.

### Mission

Our mission is to preserve, teach, and advance Coptology by building
reliable digital tools that connect language learners, researchers, teachers,
and communities to high-quality Coptic resources.

### Vision

Our vision is for Coptic Compass to become the trusted digital reference point
for Coptic language learning and Coptology: a place where grammar, lexicon,
publication, research, and technology reinforce one another.

### Brand Promise

Coptic Compass helps people move through Coptology with clarity, confidence,
and context.

### Values

| Value         | Meaning                                                                    |
| :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Stewardship   | We treat Coptic heritage, texts, and language resources with care.         |
| Scholarship   | We value accuracy, attribution, review, and transparent limitations.       |
| Accessibility | We make specialist knowledge approachable without making it shallow.       |
| Continuity    | We connect ancient language, living community, and modern tools.           |
| Craft         | We build interfaces, publications, and content with discipline and polish. |
| Service       | We serve learners, teachers, churches, researchers, and contributors.      |

### What We Are

- A Coptic-language learning platform.
- A digital dictionary and grammar environment.
- The publisher of the seven current publication catalog works.
- A publication and reference catalog.
- A research and developer surface for Coptic data and workflows.
- A serious but welcoming home for Coptology.

### What We Are Not

- A personal portfolio identity.
- A casual content blog.
- A decorative heritage brand with no product depth.
- A generic AI wrapper.
- A replacement for scholarly judgment, teachers, or source criticism.

## 03. Voice And Copy

### Brand Personality

Coptic Compass should sound scholarly, patient, precise, and warm. It should
feel like an excellent teacher with modern tools: careful with sources, generous
with explanation, and confident without being loud.

### Voice Principles

- **Clear before clever**: use direct language before poetic language.
- **Specific before grand**: name concrete tools, sources, and outcomes.
- **Inviting, not casual**: be warm without becoming slangy or unserious.
- **Scholarly, not gatekeeping**: explain specialist terms when useful.
- **Humble about AI**: present AI as assistance, not authority.

### Preferred Terms

| Prefer             | Avoid                                                     |
| :----------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------- |
| Coptic Compass     | The Wannes Portfolio                                      |
| Shenute AI         | chatbot, bot, generic AI                                  |
| Coptology          | content consumption                                       |
| grammar lessons    | courses, unless referring to a full course product        |
| publications       | products, items, merch                                    |
| contributors       | users, fans, followers when referring to project builders |
| Coptic-script text | foreign text                                              |

### Naming Rules

- Keep `Coptic Compass` in English across locales.
- Do not translate the brand name into Dutch or other languages.
- Use `Shenute AI` as the assistant name.
- Use `Kyrillos Wannes` for author/founder references.
- Retire `The Wannes Portfolio` from product-facing brand surfaces.

### Boilerplate

Short:

```text
Coptic Compass is a digital home for Coptology.
```

Medium:

```text
Coptic Compass brings together a searchable Coptic dictionary, structured
grammar lessons, publications, Shenute AI, and developer tools in one digital
platform.
```

Long:

```text
Coptic Compass is a complete digital Coptology platform for students, teachers,
researchers, developers, and Coptic communities. It unifies dictionary lookup,
grammar learning, publication access, AI-assisted study, and public APIs to make
Coptic more searchable, learnable, and usable in modern digital contexts.
```

### Copy Do And Do Not

Do:

- Use plain explanations for technical or grammatical terms.
- Say what the tool actually does.
- Keep learner-facing copy encouraging and concrete.
- Give source and limitation context where scholarly accuracy matters.

Do not:

- Overpromise AI accuracy.
- Use vague claims like "revolutionary" without evidence.
- Treat Coptic as merely aesthetic decoration.
- Use portfolio-era language for the platform identity.

## 04. Visual Identity

### Brand Identity And Symbology

The Coptic Compass logo is a navigational emblem. It represents the platform as
a fixed point of orientation for people moving through Coptic language,
heritage, publication, technology, and spiritual-cultural memory. The mark
should feel like a beacon: not decorative nostalgia, but ancient wisdom made
usable as a guide for the present.

At the center of the emblem is the Coptic letter **Ⲭ**. It is the structural and
conceptual core of the brand, carrying three connected meanings:

- **Ⲭⲏⲙⲓ**: Egypt, the ancient earthly point of origin.
- **Ⲭⲣⲓⲥⲧⲓⲁⲛⲟⲥ**: the Christian faith and tradition that sustain the Coptic
  inheritance.
- **Ⲭⲁⲧⲏⲣ**: compass, naming the brand's role as a guide for those seeking
  direction.

The surrounding forms turn the central **Ⲭ** into a celestial instrument. The
ascending stroke becomes a guiding arrow, pointing forward and upward like a
compass needle. The orbiting ankh functions as a fixed star: a sign of life,
light, and spiritual illumination within the wider path of study. The winged
sky-disk form draws from ancient Egyptian visual language, suggesting
protection, elevation, and movement through the skies.

The logo's color logic reinforces the same idea. Black refers to **Ⲭⲏⲙⲓ**, the
fertile Black Land of Egypt, while also giving the mark the depth of a dark
celestial field. Gold carries divinity, starlight, sunlight, warmth, and the
guiding light that cuts through darkness. Together, black and gold should feel
historical, cosmic, and precise.

Although the symbol carries a rich narrative, the artwork must remain clean,
vector-based, and highly functional. The emblem should work as a precise digital
mark: legible in navigation, dignified on publication surfaces, and strong
enough for brand moments without becoming ornate or difficult to reproduce.

### Logo Usage

Use the primary logo as the default representation of Coptic Compass in light
mode. Use the secondary logo only as the mode-aware dark-mode counterpart in
prominent placements such as the website navigation and homepage hero.

Current app path:

```text
/logo/Coptic_Compass_Primary.svg
/logo/Coptic_Compass_Secondary.svg
```

Do not:

- Recolor the logo in CSS.
- Crop the logo manually.
- Use the secondary logo in light mode.
- Stretch, squeeze, rotate, or add effects that alter the artwork.
- Rebuild the logo from separate shapes.
- Place the logo over busy imagery.

### Clear Space

Keep clear space around the logo equal to at least one quarter of the logo's
total height. No text, icons, borders, or decorative elements should enter that
area.

### Minimum Size

Use the logo at a size where the wordmark remains legible. For very small UI
surfaces, prefer a dedicated icon-only asset if one is created later. Until then,
use the full primary SVG carefully and avoid compressing it into unreadable
spaces.

### Color Guidance

Use white, near-white, and neutral surfaces for the day-to-day product
experience. Use dark surfaces when the content calls for depth or ceremony, not
as the default for every page.

Gold is the primary brand accent, but it should not become the default color for
every button, pill, icon, or active state. Use it for brand emphasis, logo
adjacent moments, publication/catalog surfaces, selected calls to attention, and
ceremonial dividers. Gold should feel intentional and earned.

Green is the Coptic/language accent. Use it for dictionary and grammar moments,
Coptic-script support, learning progress, success states, and places where the
interface is guiding language work. Green should carry utility and movement
rather than ceremony.

Avoid treating gold and green as interchangeable accents. When both appear on a
screen, let one lead and the other support. Most product surfaces should still be
mostly paper, surface, ink, muted text, and line.

### Typography

#### Manrope

Manrope is the primary Latin/UI brand typeface. Use it for product navigation,
headings, body copy, metadata, labels, and marketing or publication surfaces
where a contemporary sans-serif is appropriate.

Local implementation:

| File                  | Purpose                                                        |
| :-------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `src/lib/fonts.ts`    | Registers Manrope through `next/font/google` as `--font-ui`.   |
| `src/app/globals.css` | Uses `--font-ui` as the first family in the `font-sans` token. |

Recommended hierarchy:

| Style       | Size  |
| :---------- | :---- |
| Heading 1   | 64 px |
| Heading 2   | 48 px |
| Subheader 1 | 32 px |
| Subheader 2 | 24 px |

| Paragraph 2 | 16 px |

#### Antinoou

Antinoou is the Coptic-script typeface, heritage display companion, and primary
print-publication typeface. In the digital product, use it for Coptic headwords,
search input, dictionary forms, grammar examples, manuscript/OCR output, and any
UI where Coptic text must remain readable and typographically authentic.

Antinoou may also be used for select Latin-script brand display moments,
especially short titles or headings where the brand should feel rooted in
Coptic typographic tradition. Good examples include the `Coptic Compass` title
on the homepage hero, editorial title slides, publication title moments, and
short section headings in brand-forward contexts.

For printed publications, publication PDFs, cover interiors, title pages, and
editorial layouts, Antinoou is the main typeface for both Latin and Coptic
scripts. Use it for publication titles, running text, front matter, back matter,
footnotes, captions, tables, and Coptic examples so Coptic Compass publications
feel like one continuous scholarly imprint rather than a digital product
exported to paper.

Local implementation:

| File                                                        | Purpose                                         |
| :---------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------- |
| `src/fonts/AntinoouFont-1.0.6/antinoou-webfont.woff2`       | Browser normal style through `next/font/local`. |
| `src/fonts/AntinoouFont-1.0.6/antinoouitalic-webfont.woff2` | Browser italic style through `next/font/local`. |
| `src/fonts/AntinoouFont-1.0.6/antinoou-webfont.woff`        | Open Graph image rendering fallback.            |
| `src/lib/fonts.ts`                                          | Registers the `antinoou` font variable.         |
| `src/app/globals.css`                                       | Exposes `.font-coptic` and `--font-coptic`.     |

Implementation rule: apply Antinoou by default to Coptic-script content. For
Latin script in digital product UI, reserve it for short brand/display titles
and headings only. Do not use Antinoou for product navigation, product body
copy, dense UI labels, form controls, product tables, or product metadata. Print
publication interiors are the exception: there, Antinoou is the default family
for both Latin and Coptic scripts.

### Imagery And Illustration

Imagery should support study, publication, manuscripts, books, language, and
community. Avoid generic academic stock images that could belong to any
education platform. When possible, use real publication covers, interface
screens, manuscript-adjacent visuals, or carefully produced brand assets.

### Layout Principles

- Prefer calm, spacious layouts with strong hierarchy.
- Let research and learning content remain the focus.
- Use cards for repeated items, tools, or framed content, not as decoration.
- Keep navigation predictable and product-like.
- Avoid visual effects that make the platform feel like a generic portfolio.

## 05. Product Experience

### Product Principles

| Principle       | Meaning                                                                 |
| :-------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Searchable      | Users should be able to find language information quickly.              |
| Teachable       | Explanations should support real learning, not just lookup.             |
| Trustworthy     | Data, limitations, and review states should be visible where relevant.  |
| Bilingual-aware | English and Dutch surfaces should preserve brand names and core terms.  |
| Coptic-first    | Coptic text rendering, input, and readability are product requirements. |
| Developer-ready | APIs and technical workflows should be documented and stable.           |

### Navigation

Primary navigation should emphasize the platform pillars:

- Publications
- Grammar
- Dictionary
- Shenute AI

Dictionary owns related tools such as Search, Practice, and Analytics rather
than exposing each tool as a separate top-level item.

Contact, account, dashboard, and admin surfaces should feel connected to the
same brand without overpowering learning and reference tasks. In the website
header, Contact and authentication belong with the right-side utility actions
rather than the main platform-pillar group.

### AI Experience

Shenute AI should be presented as a study assistant. It can help explain,
search, summarize, and guide, but it should not be framed as a final scholarly
authority. The product should maintain clear boundaries around source quality,
retrieval, and generated answers.

### Accessibility

- Maintain strong color contrast, especially for gold on light surfaces.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate state.
- Keep Coptic text large enough for detailed reading.
- Preserve keyboard navigation and visible focus states.
- Use alt text for brand imagery and publication covers.

## 06. Applications

### Website

The website is the primary brand environment. It should feel like a real product
and research platform, not a landing page for a single person. The logo, name,
navigation, metadata, and copy should consistently use Coptic Compass.

### Publications

Publication surfaces may use stronger ceremonial brand treatments. Gold and dark
brand variants are appropriate here when they support the seriousness of the
material.

### Documentation

Technical documentation should be precise and plain. It should help developers
understand the public API, OCR flows, grammar data, dictionary data, and Shenute
AI workflows without marketing language getting in the way.

### Social And Open Graph

Social previews should make the brand immediately recognizable. Use the primary
logo, clear title text, and concise descriptions. Avoid cluttered compositions
or screenshots that become illegible at small sizes.

### Presentations

Presentation decks should use the logo with generous clear space, large type,
and restrained color. Use gold and dark surfaces for title or section-divider
moments, then return to readable neutral content slides.

## 07. Governance

### Asset Rules

- Store brand logo assets in `public/logo`.
- Preserve approved asset filenames once code references them.
- Keep SVG and PNG variants together.
- Do not overwrite original logo artwork with generated derivatives unless a new
  export is approved.
- Update this brand book whenever filenames, colors, typography, or usage rules
  change.

### Implementation Checklist

When changing brand assets or core identity code:

- Confirm the referenced logo file exists in `public/logo`.
- Regenerate app icons with `npm run assets:icon` when square icon sources
  change.
- Check markdown asset links in this guide.
- Run formatting on this document.
- Run `npm run lint`.
- Run `npm run typecheck`.
- Visually check the navbar and homepage hero when a dev server is available.
